## Summary

## Full Journal Entry

After Beta, once Qwrk is working very well, I see a clear strategic path opening.

By that point, I will have used Qwrk extensively to help me implement real projects — not by relying on Qwrk itself, but by orchestrating work through CustomGPTs and their native file storage and workflows. In doing so, I’ll have proven the approach, not just the idea.

With that proof in hand, I could initiate a focused conversation with the right individual at Resolve — or potentially another, currently unidentified partner.

The conversation would be simple and grounded in results: I built a better way to manage projects, work, and knowledge — one that feels natural, adaptive, and deeply supportive of daily work. A system that captures “seeds” and allows them to grow into projects, supports collaboration, reads email context, watches calendars, schedules meetings, creates documents, and evolves alongside the user.

I could propose licensing my design to provide Rita with Qwrk-like capabilities — initially as an internal product, but potentially as a powerful enhancement to Rita itself.

The potential feels expansive. The value is clear if proven. The opportunity is not to run a traditional software company, but to build something elegant, practical, and quietly transformative — and let it find the right home.
