# Weekly Qwrk Stewardship Loop

## Purpose
This framework governs how Joel invests attention, design effort, and build energy into Qwrk on a weekly basis. Its purpose is to ensure Qwrk compounds long-term trust, coherence, and judgment rather than drifting toward urgency, novelty, or over-construction.

This is a stewardship framework, not a productivity system. It exists to protect Qwrk from becoming busy instead of useful, clever instead of trustworthy, or complex instead of calm.

---

## Core Principle

Qwrk evolves best when it is treated as a living system that is stewarded intentionally, not a tool that is constantly improved reactively.

Each week must answer one question clearly:

**What single investment in Qwrk will compound its long-term usefulness and trust?**

---

## The Weekly Loop

The Weekly Qwrk Stewardship Loop consists of three decisions, made once per week.

### 1. Choose the Compounding Axis

Select exactly one primary axis for the week:

- **Memory** — What Qwrk remembers, forgets, retrieves, or ignores
- **Governance** — Rules that constrain the other four axes. Not a catch-all for uncategorized work
- **Execution** — Pipeline reliability: Gateway, workflows, determinism, plumbing. Excludes intent fidelity
- **Interface** — How naturally Joel interacts with Qwrk. High-dopamine; must not become the default weekly choice
- **Judgment** — Quality of Qwrk's autonomous decisions when Joel is not explicitly directing. Grounded in testable behaviors: classification, refusal, clarification, slowing, enforcement

Only one axis may be active per week. Multiple axes indicate hedging and lead to drift.

Secondary axis effects are acceptable; secondary axis goals are not. During a given week, work may incidentally touch other axes, but only one axis may have explicit goals.

---

### 2. Run the Qwrk Impact Test

Any proposed change, behavior, rule, or build must pass at least two of the following three tests:

1. **Clarity Test** — Will this make future conversations shorter or clearer?
2. **Load Test** — Will this reduce Joel’s cognitive load rather than increase it?
3. **Stewardship Test** — Does this move Qwrk toward being a steward rather than a servant?

If a proposal fails two of three tests, it is deferred without debate.

---

### 3. Lock the Weekly Qwrk Bet

Choose exactly one Weekly Qwrk Bet.

A valid bet:
- Is observable by the end of the week
- Changes how Qwrk behaves or is used
- Improves trust, coherence, or calm

Examples:
- Stabilize how rolling memory is enforced
- Clarify when Qwrk should slow urgency
- Simplify how artifacts are suggested or saved

Documentation alone is not a valid bet unless it directly alters behavior.

---

## No-Build Boundary

Each week must explicitly declare what will **not** be built, taught, or refined in Qwrk.

Examples:
- No new artifact types
- No new modes
- No performance tuning
- No abstraction cleanup
- No speculative UI work

The No-Build Boundary exists to prevent premature intelligence and system fragility.

---

## Friday Steward Review

At the end of the week, review using only these two questions:

1. **Is Qwrk more trustworthy than it was on Monday?**
2. **Did this week reduce future decision fatigue?**

If either answer is no, the bet was wrong. This is information, not failure.

---

## Relationship to Governance

- This document is authoritative doctrine but not runtime memory
- Enforcement is handled via a separate Monday governance rule
- This document may be superseded but must never be silently edited

---

## Closing Note

A simpler Qwrk that is trusted beats a smarter Qwrk that is not.

Stewardship over speed. Governance before capability. Calm over cleverness.
